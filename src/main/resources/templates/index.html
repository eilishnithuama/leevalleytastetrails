Tours

Lee Valley Taste Trail is a small group tour business for visitors to the South West region of Co. Cork. We offer a unique opportunity to join, or book, your group to experience the flavour of the famous Lee river basin. Meet award winning Artisan producers and be amazed by its rich heritage and cultural environments. All with a local Failte Ireland Tour Guide/Chef in a small luxury bus.

Spend the day with us on our bespoke trail, have great fun with relaxed and professional insight into this beautiful yet vibrant part of Ireland. We have two distinctive trail itineraries devised for you to book. Dorothy will take you on a leisurely tour around this rich and fertile river valley to give you a exclusive and informative snapshot of the area. We hope you will love it as much as we do.

About Your Guide and Driver!

Dorothy has a wealth of experience working in the hospitality / Food Tourism industry. She is well travelled, from home in Inchigeela, to San Francisco, Brussels and the Beara Pennisula! Dorothy has worked in many award winning hotels and restaurants including her own in the past.

Dorothy has gained extensive training and knowledge of Tour Guiding, Irish Food History and Culture with lots of cooking techniques and tips exclusive to the River Lee Valley.

Your driver, Patrick Creedon, runs his family business and has a long tradition in providing a reliable taxi and mini-bus service country wide. He prides himself on keeping well maintained buses and ensuring dependability on your journey.

The bus is a black luxury 16 seater Mercedes and is wheelchair accessible. He and his family live locally in Ballingeary.

Tour 2

Taste Our Stored Sunshine

Tour Breakdown

  • B
    History of Macroom Castle Tower and Demesne Grounds
    Macroom Castle was built under the reign of King John is the early 13th century. It was home to various Irish waring cheiftans. It was then given to Admiral William Penn (father of William Penn Jnr, founder of Pennsylvannia, USA) by Cromwell in the 17th century.
  • C
    Visit to Waltons Flour Mills
    Meet the 6th generation miller of Macroom Oatmeal and stone-ground flour. The mill has been in use since 1832 and is a big hit in Ballymaloe where it is used for their porridge everyday.
  • DE
    Coolavokig Pottery and Carrigaphooca Castle
    See artisan potters using the oldest wood-fired kiln in Ireland and browse their handcrafted ceramic wares in their pottery shop. Carrigaphooca Castle was built in the 15th Century and is said one of the most haunted castles in Ireland.
  • F
    St. Gobnait's Cemetery and 6th century Monastic Ruins
    St. Gobnait is the patron saint of Bee Keepers. A story is told that an angel instructed her, that if she came upon 9 white deer in Ireland, that would be her place of resurrection. She saw them in Ballyvourney and built her monastery there.
  • G
    Lunch stop at the The Mills Inn and Gardens in Ballyvourney
    The Mills Inn was established in 1755 and is a family run business. Enjoy their thatched roof craft shop (An Siopa) and old Mill Museum. At this stop you can reserve a delicious pub lunch, be introduced to native Irish speakers and enjoy some traditional Irish music.
  • H
    Private tour of the 9 White Deer Craft Brewery with Master Brewer
    Meet Gordon Lucey or Don O'Leary, owners of the 9 White Deer brewery. Beer tasting is included as well as free bottle of your favourite to take away.
    (Minimum 10 persons required)

Information regarding Tour

  • Dorothy will meet and brief the group at 9am at the Castle Hotel reception and the tour will return to the Castle hotel at 4pm later that day. This is marked by a 1 on the above map.
  • Lunch and drinks are not included but delicious picnics or group pub lunches can be pre-booked 24hrs prior to set off.
  • Layered clothing / walking shoes, wellingtons or boots are advisable. A small day-pack can be brought on the bus.
  • Payment in full is required prior prior to set off and can be taken by Dorothy.
  • Please inform of any food allergy requirements or health concerns prior to set off also.

Tour 1

Meet the Buffalos in the South West

Tour Breakdown

  • B
    Stroll through Macrooms historical town square
    Short guided stroll of the historical buildings therein and experience the taste trail of the buzzing Tuesday farmers market.
  • C
    Walk through Gearagh Nature Reserve
    Walk on water through this spectacular alluvial oak woodland and nature reserve on the river Lee. It hosts a large diversity of wildfowl and attracts both summer and winter migrants.
  • D
    Private visit to Macroom Buffalo Mozzarella farm
    Visit Irelands first and only herd of milking water buffalo. Meet the farmer Johnny and his buffalos. View the production dairy and sample the variety of cheeses including mozzarella, ricotta, haloumi and greek salad cheese.
  • E
    Picnic Lunch in Store of Memories Museum
    The Store of Memories museum is a well preserved example of last centuries unique Irish village culture. It traded in fresh food, groceries, hardware and fuel from 1910 to 2007 and is built over the Derrivane river. A brown bread and homemade butter demonstration will be given by Dorothy using Macroom Mill Stone-ground flour. Pre-booked picnics are available or you can bring your own.
  • F
    Lake side drive of the 7 lakes Loch Allua
    View the historic Irish archaeological features such as a mass rock, a crann√≥g, standing stones along the river. The 7 chained lakes of the river Lee, called Loch Allua, rank amoung the top pike fishing lakes in Ireland.
  • G
    Tour of Gougane Barra Forest park and walk around St. Finbarr's Oratory
    This forest park is Ireland's first national park. It was opened in 1964 and is the source of the Lee. The oratory is located on a small island in the middle of Gougane Barra lake and is the site of St. Finbarr's 6th century monastic hermitage.
